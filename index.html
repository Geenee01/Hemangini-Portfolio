Statistical analysis and modelling for colorectal liver metastasis data

This project was developed with clinical data and CT scan data of patients who underwent a surgery for treatment of colorectal liver metastasis. Among these patients majority of them had multiple tumors. The data includes CT scans with corresponding clinical data which contains follow up and survival information of patients. When dealing with multiple tumors, there is a need of aggregating the radiomic features from all tumors present in one patients. The goal was to aggregate the radiomic features in patients with multiple tumors in mathematical methods and compare them usinf statistical methods and machine learning models to find out better performing method on survival analysis.

Radiomic-based approaches in the multi-metastatic setting: a quantitative review

This project was part of collaboration between Queen's University's Biomedical Computing Lab and Medical Biophysics Lab of UHN, where I worked as Data Analyst Intern. In this project, I worked with metastasis data for different location in the body and conducted research for ten aggregation methods and survival analysis. This reserach article published in BMC cancer journal in March 2025.
